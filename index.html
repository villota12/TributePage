The Phenom: Alyssa Valdez

The Most Famous Volleyball Player in the Philippines

Volleyball Journey

Early Years

  • 1993: Born on June 29 in San Juan, Batangas, Philippines. Alyssa grew up in a sports-oriented family, sparking her interest in volleyball early.

Highschool Career

  • 2008-2011: Attended UST High School, where she became a standout volleyball player in the UAAP Girls’ Division. Her impressive skills helped the team secure multiple championships and earned her MVP awards.

Ateneo de Manila University (UAAP)

  • 2011-2016: Played for the Ateneo Lady Eagles and became one of the UAAP's most celebrated athletes.
    • 2012: Named Rookie of the Year, making an immediate impact on her team.
    • 2014: Led Ateneo to its first UAAP championship, ending a long drought for the Lady Eagles. She was awarded Season MVP.
    • 2015: Helped Ateneo secure a back-to-back championship, solidifying her status as one of the best players in the league. She was again awarded the Season MVP.
    • 2016: Ended her UAAP career as one of the league's all-time scoring leaders and became widely regarded as the "Phenom."

Professional Career

  • 2016: Joined the Philippine Super Liga (PSL) with the Bureau of Customs Transformers team, helping promote professional volleyball in the Philippines.
  • 2017-2023: Became part of the Premier Volleyball League (PVL), representing the Creamline Cool Smashers.
    • 2018: Led the Creamline Cool Smashers to their first PVL title. She continued to shine as one of the league’s most prominent players.
    • 2019-2022: Guided the Cool Smashers to multiple PVL titles, reinforcing her legacy as one of the top players in Philippine volleyball.
    • 2022: Helped the Cool Smashers capture the PVL Open Conference title, marking their fourth championship together.

National Team

  • 2015: Represented the Philippines at the Southeast Asian (SEA) Games, showcasing her skills on an international stage.
    • 2017: Rejoined the Philippine national team, competing in various SEA Games and Asian Volleyball Confederation (AVC) tournaments.
  • 2019: Competed in the SEA Games held in the Philippines, with massive local support.
  • 2022: Once again represented the Philippines in the SEA Games.

Present and Legacy

  • 2023: Continues to play for the Creamline Cool Smashers, inspiring younger players and volleyball fans across the country.
  • Beyond Volleyball: Alyssa has become a household name, contributing to the sport as a brand ambassador and media personality and promoting volleyball through various platforms.
